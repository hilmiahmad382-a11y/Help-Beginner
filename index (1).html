🌟 Intermediate English Quiz

1. When a business associate greets you with "How do you do?", what is culturally considered the most appropriate formal response?
A. I'm doing great, thanks for asking!
B. How do you do?
C. I am a manager at my firm.
D. Not much, what about you?
2. If a colleague greets you casually by asking, "How's tricks?", what are they inquiring about?
A. Your favorite card game or magic tricks
B. How your day or general life is going
C. Technical errors with office equipment
D. A request for a favor
3. Which phrase is an idiomatic equivalent to "Long time no see" but carries a slightly warmer, more expressive tone?
A. It's been an absolute age!
B. Goodbye for now.
C. Pleased to make your acquaintance.
D. Speak of the devil.
4. Someone greets you in passing with "Alright?". What is the most appropriate native-like reaction?
A. No, I am experiencing several problems.
B. Nod and say "Yeah, alright?" right back.
C. Stand still and explain your evening schedule.
D. Say "Thank you very much."
5. In a formal networking environment, which phrase works best to transition from a greeting into explaining your purpose?
A. Yo, let's cut right to the chase.
B. It’s a pleasure to connect with you. I wanted to follow up on...
C. What's new with you? Listen to this...
D. How's life treating you? Anyway...
6. What does the greeting phrase "How are you holding up?" implicitly indicate?
A. The speaker wants to know if you can lift something heavy.
B. The speaker knows you've been going through a difficult situation.
C. The speaker is greeting a complete stranger.
D. The speaker wants to know your financial standing.
7. If someone says "It’s wonderful to finally see you in the flesh!", what does it mean?
A. They are meeting you in person after only communicating online.
B. They are making a comment about your clothing choices.
C. They think you look very tired or unhealthy.
D. They are angry that you arrived late.
8. Select the phrase that represents a highly professional, respectful greeting for a formal cover letter where the recipient's name is unknown:
A. To Whom It May Concern
B. Hey Hiring Team
C. Dear Hiring Manager
D. Yo! Good morning
9. What is the most natural reply to the casual greeting phrase "What have you been up to?"
A. Yes, I have been up there.
B. Just the usual, keeping busy with work.
C. I am 175 centimeters tall.
D. Fine, thank you, and you?
10. Which option represents a polite way to greet an unexpected guest while expressing pleasant surprise?
A. What brings you here? Explain yourself.
B. What a pleasant surprise! Come on in.
C. I wasn't expecting you at all today.
D. Hello, why didn't you text me first?
11. If a business client starts an email with "I hope this email finds you well," what type of tone does this set?
A. Archaic and completely incorrect
B. Standard, courteous corporate correspondence
C. Highly casual and intended only for close friends
D. Urgent and demanding a quick reply
12. When someone leaves a gathering and says, "Give my best to your family," what are they requesting?
A. A financial gift for their household
B. That you pass on their warm regards and greetings to them
C. To borrow a family member's vehicle
D. To complain about the behavior of your family
13. Which response works best when someone greets you with "Nice to see you" if you have already met them previously?
A. Nice to meet you too.
B. Great to see you again too!
C. Who are you exactly?
D. Same here, stranger.
14. What does the common parting greeting "Take care" communicate?
A. A warning about an immediate physical hazard nearby
B. A warm, well-wishing farewell indicating safety and health
C. A strict instruction to guard personal belongings
D. An invitation to look after someone else's children
15. If a business meeting is wrapping up, which expression is most professional to use as a closing statement?
A. Alright, I'm out of here. Catch you later.
B. Thank you all for your time today. I look forward to our next steps.
C. Bye, don't forget to send me money.
D. Peace out, guys. Have fun.
